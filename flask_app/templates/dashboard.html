How It Works

Hangry works to quickly provide you restaurant options to solve your hanger.

Hangry allows you to save your frequent locations such as work and home. You can add a new location by clicking HERE. Then, you can add some of you favorite go-to restaurants by entering the restaurant's address and selecting which location you want to add that favorite to by clicking HERE.

After adding some of your locations and favorites associated with those locations, Hangry can help you decide where to eat. You will first have the option to select which one of your locations you are deciding to eat from. Then you can narrow down your preferences by deselecting favorites you are not interested in. About Us

Our Story

"Hanger"

pronunciation: haNGÉ™r

Noun: a feeling of anger and frustration caused by hunger

As someone who often gets lost in work and forgets to eat as a result, I frequently experience hanger. It affects my ability to think clearly and I begin to get snippy with the people around me. The real problem arises when deciding where to eat. The longer it takes me to decide the more irritable I become!

This is where the idea for Hangry was born. Here at Hangry, our mission isn't to find you a new amazing spot to eat. We know that you need food, you need it fast, and you probably already have a few go-to spots nearby where you would want to eat. This is where we come in to help! Instead of letting your jumbled, hanger-soaked mind attempt to figure out where to eat as your hanger grows worse and worse by the second, we are here to quickly make that decision for you!

Future of Hangry

Hangry's main functionality is built out but there are many other places to improve and develop. One new development to be implemented would be to provide a user with a new restaurant based on their location that many other users frequent. Ideally, in the distant future, Hangry will be able to incorporate minimal forms of machine learning and AI to give users new suggestions and to automatically add frequented restaurants to their favorites without the user having to manually input them.

Additionally, in terms of front-end development, the app's look could use a facelift to make it more appealing and engaging to users rather than just focused on providing the bare bones structure to make Hangry function properly.

Similarly, in regards to back-end development, much of the code can and should be revised to improve efficiency and decrease repeated work. Reworking the code would make it easier to maintain and update for future changes. The CSS and HTML could also be redesigned in a similar fashion to make it less repetitive and easier to update.
